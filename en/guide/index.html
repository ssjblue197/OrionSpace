Quick Start

First-time Setup

Orino Studio needs to download some AI components to your machine before use. This process only runs once.

1
Download and install Orino Studio
Download the installer for your operating system (Windows / macOS). Run the installer and launch the application.
2
Create an account
Sign up with email or Google. Every new account gets a 1-day free trial with full access to all features.
3
Run the Setup Wizard
The app will guide you through installing required resources. Click "Install" and wait for completion — typically 2–5 minutes depending on your internet speed.
4
Download AI resources (as needed)
Go to Settings → Engine to download additional AI resources. Not required immediately — only needed when using Translation or Voice AI features.
Orino Studio runs completely offline after setup. No video data is ever sent to any server.
Video Download

Downloading Videos

Supports YouTube, TikTok, Bilibili, Douyin, Facebook, Kuaishou, Xiaohongshu, iQiyi, and hundreds of other sources. Download video, thumbnails, and subtitles individually or all at once.

Three ways to add a video

By URL
Paste a video link directly. The app auto-detects the title, uploader, and available quality options for that video.
Keyword Search
Search YouTube or Bilibili directly in the app. Browse results with thumbnails, duration, and view count.
Batch URL List
Import a .txt file with one URL per line. All videos are queued and downloaded in parallel.

Download options

QualityChoose from available resolutions (4K, 1080p, 720p, etc.) including separate audio-only downloads.
FormatMP4, MKV, WebM, or audio-only (MP3).
SubtitlesDownload platform subtitles (.srt / .vtt) independently, with optional timeline normalization.
ThumbnailDownload video thumbnail in original resolution.
Clip trimDownload only a portion of a video by setting start and end timestamps.
PlaylistDownload an entire playlist or channel in one go.
SponsorBlockAutomatically cut sponsor segments from YouTube videos.
MetadataEmbed title and uploader info into the file; keep the original video title as the filename.
ThreadsAdjust the number of parallel connections for faster downloads.

Download queue

The floating button in the corner shows all active downloads. From there you can track per-video progress, cancel or retry, and open finished files directly.

For videos that require login (members-only, age-restricted), use the Cookie feature. See the Cookies & Gated Content section below.
Translation & Dubbing

Translating & Dubbing a Video

The translation pipeline is fully automatic: speech recognition → translation → subtitle generation → voice synthesis → video export. Each step can be reviewed and adjusted.

Pipeline steps

1
Upload & configure
Select your video, choose source and target language, then pick the output mode: Subtitles, Dubbing, or both — plus an optional watermark.
2
Transcript (speech or hard-subs)
Whisper ASR segments the speech into timestamped subtitles (99+ languages, quality tiers from Fast to Best). For videos with burned-in subtitles, switch the source to OCR — the app reads on-screen text instead, keeping the original timeline.
3
Translation
Three engines: Offline (no internet), Online (Google), or external AI (OpenAI / Claude). Review and edit any line in the bilingual subtitle editor before proceeding.
4
Subtitle & voice generation
Subtitles are rendered with your chosen preset. Voice synthesis generates dubbed audio with two engines: Fast (CPU-friendly) or Premium (highest quality).
5
Export
The final video is exported with burned-in subtitles, dubbed audio, and background music (if AI separation was enabled). Use Re-export to change styling or audio without re-translating.

Subtitle editor

EditFix any line side-by-side with the original; split or merge segments; click a line to seek the video.
TimelineTimeline Editor for adjusting timecodes visually and inserting subtitles at any point.
AI EnhanceRe-translate and polish all lines with your connected AI (adds natural phrasing and expressive cues for dubbing).
SRT filesImport original or pre-translated .srt/.vtt (skips transcription/translation), export both, and offset timelines by seconds.
CPS alertReading-speed warnings when a line is too long for its duration.

Hard-sub masking (OCR source)

MaskingHide the original burned-in subtitles: AI inpaint (erase), blur, or dark background — or place new subs exactly over the old ones.
Scan areaAuto-detect, draw the region on the video, or pick bottom 15% / 25% / top 15%.
ContinuousKeep the mask on even between subtitle lines to avoid flicker.

Dubbing & export options

BackgroundReplace the original audio entirely, keep it underneath, or keep only the music (AI vocal separation) with adjustable volume.
DenoiseClean up cloned-voice output before mixing.
AspectOriginal, 9:16 vertical, 1:1 square, or 4:3 — plus flip, zoom, brightness/contrast/saturation, and the Ken Burns effect.
WatermarkCustom image or text stamp on export. Trial accounts always export with the default watermark.

Subtitle presets

Choose from 16 built-in presets (Neon, Classic, Minimal, Bold, Mint, Rose, and more). Adjust font size, color, position, and opacity. Preview renders live on the video.

Already have a subtitle file? Drag a .srt or .vtt file into the transcript attachment field to skip speech recognition and go straight to translation.
Voice AI

Voice Creation & Cloning

Three modes for generating speech: standard TTS synthesis, voice cloning from a short sample, and voice design from a text description.

TTS Synthesis
Type text or import an .srt to speak along its timecodes. Two engines — Fast (CPU-friendly) and Premium (best quality) — with 13 nonverbal emotion tags (laugh, sigh, surprise, etc.) and inline pronunciation editing.
Voice Clone
Upload 1–3 samples of 3–10 seconds or record directly in the app, with optional noise/music separation. The engine learns the voice and reproduces it in any content or language.
Voice Design
Describe a voice (gender, age, pitch, style) in text. The engine generates a voice matching your description.

Voice library

BrowseGrid view with a sidebar to filter by language and provider; search by name and sort A–Z or by date.
HardwareFilter voices by what runs best on your machine: CPU-friendly, GPU-optimized, or both.
GroupsCloned and built-in voices are grouped separately; pick any voice for TTS or video dubbing.
.gxvoiceExport one or many voices into a single .gxvoice file for backup, and import them on another device.
Settings & Engine

Engine Configuration

AI processing happens locally on your machine. Choose between GPU (NVIDIA CUDA) and CPU modes, and manage which AI models are installed.

GPU ModeUses your NVIDIA GPU via CUDA. Significantly faster — recommended if you have a supported GPU.
CPU ModeWorks on any computer. Slower than GPU but all features are fully functional.
ModelsDownload speech recognition, subtitle OCR, translation, and voice models independently. Only install what you need.
StorageModels and outputs are stored locally. View total size and clean up per category (translated videos, generated voices, downloads, recordings).
WatermarkConfigure a custom image or text watermark: position (corners/center), size, and opacity.
UpdatesClick "Check for updates" in Settings — when a new version is available, install and restart in one click.
GPU status is displayed in the status bar at the bottom of the app. If it shows CPU, make sure the NVIDIA CUDA drivers are up to date and GPU mode is enabled in Settings → Engine.

Advanced

Cookies & Gated Content

Some videos require a logged-in session to download (members-only, age-restricted, private playlists). Orino Studio can read cookies from your browser to access this content.

1
Log in to the platform in your browser
Make sure you're signed in to the platform (YouTube, Bilibili, etc.) in Chrome, Firefox, or Edge.
2
Open Cookie settings in the app
In the Video Download screen, click the Cookie button and select your browser from the list.
3
Download normally
The app reads your session credentials and can now download content that requires authentication.
Chrome must be fully closed for its cookies to be readable. Alternatively, export a cookies.txt file with the "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY" browser extension and import it in the app — handy for reuse without opening the browser.
Advanced

External AI Translation

Translation, the "AI Enhance" button in the subtitle editor, and TTS text enhancement can all use OpenAI (GPT) or Anthropic (Claude) for higher quality than the offline engine — especially on specialized content.

ConnectGo to Settings → AI Connection and pick OpenAI (ChatGPT) or Anthropic (Claude). The app opens your browser to sign in on the provider's official page — no manual API key needed.
ModelsAfter connecting, pick the exact model in Settings (latest GPT and Claude model lines are available).
UsageEach translated or enhanced subtitle segment consumes a small amount of your provider account's usage.
When using external AI, subtitle text is sent to that provider's API. This data does not pass through Orino's servers.
